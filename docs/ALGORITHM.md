# How the Algorithm Works (V3)

This document explains every classification rule in Honest Opens V3, what signal it uses, why it works, what confidence level it carries, and how it was validated against ground truth.

V3 was validated against raw Sailthru event data using the ESP's `is_real` flag as ground truth across 700,000+ click sends and 9 million+ open sends.

---

## The Core Idea

Every time an email is sent to a subscriber, the ESP records "events" — open-pixel fires and link clicks. Some of those events are generated by humans reading the email. Many are generated by machines: corporate email security scanners that pre-click every link, Apple Mail Privacy Protection that pre-fetches every open pixel, and enterprise proxies that crawl email content before delivering it to the inbox.

The algorithm examines the **timing, volume, and behavioral pattern** of events on each individual send and classifies them as human or bot. It does not use IP addresses, User-Agent strings, or any personally identifiable information. It works entirely on timestamps and event counts.

---

## V3 Changes from V2

V3 was the first version validated against ESP ground truth. The validation exposed two rules that were 100% wrong and one bot rule that was catching too many humans:

| Change | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Removed `thoughtful_multi`** | Was assigned 78% human probability. Actual human rate: 0%. Every send classified by this rule was a bot — delayed re-scans happening days apart that mimicked "thoughtful" behavior. |
| **Removed `single_moderate`** | Was assigned 72% human probability. Actual human rate: 0%. Bots with a 2-minute delay in a narrow 121-131 second timing band. |
| **Added ESP-rescue for `high_volume`** | The `high_volume` bot rule (10+ clicks) had a 45.5% actual human rate — nearly half the sends it blocked were real. V3 rescues sends where the ESP's `is_real` flag disagrees AND timing is late. |
| **Added ESP-rescue for `url_scanner`** | Similar rescue logic for the URL scanner rule. |
| **Added timing-only rescue** | For high-volume clicks arriving 1+ hour after send with moderate click counts, rescue even without an ESP flag. |

## Open Classification Rules

Opens are classified in priority order. The first matching rule wins.

### HUMAN:verified_clicker *(definitive confidence, probability: 99)*

**Signal:** The subscriber also clicked a link on this email, and that click was classified as human.

**Logic:** If you clicked, you opened. This is the strongest possible corroboration. The click classification runs first, and if it returns human, the open is automatically verified.

**Calibration:** Assigned 99%, actual 94.6% against Sailthru ground truth. The 5.4% gap is because some clicks classified as human by our algorithm are classified as NHI by Sailthru — a disagreement between our rules and the ESP's, not necessarily an error.

### HUMAN:esp_confirmed *(high confidence, probability: 88)*

**Signal:** The ESP's own internal bot filter flagged at least one open event on this send as "real" (non-bot).

**Logic:** ESPs like Sailthru separate opens into "real" and "NHI" (non-human interaction) categories using their own proprietary signals (User-Agent analysis, IP reputation, behavioral models). If the ESP says it is real, we trust that as a strong signal.

**Calibration:** Assigned 85%, actual 100%. This rule is under-confident — every send the ESP confirms as real is genuinely real.

**Availability:** Only fires if your ESP provides an NHI/bot flag on open events.

### BOT:instant_prefetch *(high confidence, probability: 5)*

**Signal:** The first open event fired within seconds of the email being sent (default: 10 seconds), and there were at most 2 opens with minimal time span between them.

**Logic:** No human reads an email within 10 seconds of it being sent. These are Apple Mail Privacy Protection pre-fetches or enterprise email security tools that render email content for scanning.

**Threshold:** `bot_instant_max_seconds` (default: 10.0s). Calibrated on Sailthru data where bot instant opens cluster at 7-9 seconds.

### BOT:bot_click_session *(medium confidence, probability: 10)*

**Signal:** The open occurred within a short window of clicks that were classified as bot.

**Logic:** If a security scanner clicked every link in the email (classified as BOT:machinegun), any open that fired during that same scanning session is also bot activity.

**Threshold:** `bot_session_window_seconds` (default: 120s).

### HUMAN:apple_mail_double *(medium confidence, probability: 70)*

**Signal:** Exactly 2 open events on this send, with a meaningful time gap between them.

**Logic:** Apple Mail Privacy Protection fires one automated open when the email is delivered. When the subscriber actually reads the email later, a second open fires. This "double open" pattern — one fast, one delayed — is a strong indicator of a real reader behind an Apple device.

**Calibration note:** Sailthru classifies all Apple Mail opens as NHI, so this rule shows 0% actual human rate against ESP ground truth. However, subscribers caught by this rule have an average of 83 verified opens in their history and 29% core engagement rates. They are clearly real — the ESP ground truth is wrong here, not our algorithm.

**Threshold:** `apple_double_exact_opens` (default: 2), `apple_double_min_span_seconds` (default: 5.0s).

### HUMAN:multi_open *(medium confidence, probability: 75)*

**Signal:** 3 or more open events spread over a meaningful time span.

**Logic:** Bots typically fire one open (or a rapid burst). Humans re-open emails — they read it in the morning, come back to it after lunch, forward it to a colleague.

**Calibration note:** Same Apple Mail caveat. Subscribers in this rule average 132 verified opens in history and 52% core engagement rates.

**Threshold:** `multi_open_min_events` (default: 3), `multi_open_min_span_seconds` (default: 300s / 5 minutes).

### HUMAN:reopen_long_span *(medium confidence, probability: 72)*

**Signal:** 2 or more opens with a very long time span between first and last.

**Logic:** Someone opened the email, then came back to it much later. Median span in calibration data was approximately 25 hours.

**Threshold:** `reopen_min_span_seconds` (default: 14,400s / 4 hours).

### BOT:never_verified_fast *(medium confidence, probability: 15)*

**Signal:** The subscriber has never had a verified human open across their entire history (at least 5 sends), and this open was fast (under 30 seconds).

**Logic:** Some email servers have persistent bot scanners that fire open pixels on every incoming email. If a subscriber has received many emails and never once had a corroborated open, and their opens are consistently fast, the pattern is bot.

**Threshold:** `bot_never_verified_min_history` (default: 5 sends), `bot_never_verified_max_seconds` (default: 30s).

### UNCERTAIN:no_evidence *(low confidence, probability: 40)*

**Signal:** A single open event with no corroborating signals in either direction.

**Logic:** This is the gray zone. A single open pixel fired, but there is no click to verify it, no ESP real/NHI flag, no multi-open pattern, and the timing is ambiguous. This is overwhelmingly Apple Mail Privacy Protection traffic.

**Treatment:** `unique_open = False`, but `estimated_open = True`. Use `unique_open` for advertiser-facing metrics and `estimated_open` for internal audience sizing.

## Click Classification Rules

Clicks are classified in priority order. Bot rules are checked first, then ESP-rescue rules can override, then human rules fire.

### BOT:instant_prefetch *(definitive confidence, probability: 1)*

**Signal:** The first click occurred within 5 seconds of the email being sent.

**Logic:** Enterprise security tools (Barracuda, Mimecast, Proofpoint, Microsoft ATP) pre-click every link in an email to scan for malware before delivering it to the inbox.

**Calibration:** Assigned 1%, actual 3.6%. Well-calibrated.

**Threshold:** `bot_instant_definitive_max_seconds` (default: 5.0s).

### BOT:instant_likely *(high confidence, probability: 3)*

**Signal:** The first click occurred within 5-10 seconds of send.

**Calibration:** Assigned 3%, actual 3.1%. Extremely well-calibrated.

**Threshold:** `bot_instant_likely_max_seconds` (default: 10.0s).

### BOT:machinegun *(definitive confidence, probability: 1)*

**Signal:** Multiple distinct URLs were clicked with an average inter-click time under 0.5 seconds.

**Logic:** Security scanners click every link in rapid succession. Median inter-click time in calibration data: 0.125 seconds (125 milliseconds).

**Calibration:** Assigned 1%, actual 0.3%. Well-calibrated.

**Threshold:** `bot_machinegun_definitive_max_inter_click` (default: 0.5s), `bot_machinegun_min_urls` (default: 3).

### BOT:machinegun_likely *(high confidence, probability: 5)*

**Signal:** Multiple URLs clicked with inter-click time between 0.5 and 1.0 seconds.

**Calibration:** Assigned 5%, actual 2.7%. Well-calibrated.

### BOT:url_scanner *(high confidence, probability: 5)*

**Signal:** 5+ distinct URLs clicked with 10+ total click events.

**V3 ESP-rescue:** If the ESP says at least one click was real AND the average inter-click time exceeds 20 seconds, the send is rescued as human. This addresses the 10.2% of url_scanner sends that are actually real.

**Threshold:** `bot_scanner_min_urls` (default: 5), `bot_scanner_min_total_clicks` (default: 10).

### BOT:high_volume *(high confidence, probability: 8)*

**Signal:** 10+ total click events on a single send.

**V3 ESP-rescue:** This was the most miscalibrated bot rule — 45.5% of sends it classified as bot were actually human per Sailthru. V3 adds two rescue paths:
1. **ESP rescue:** If the ESP says real AND the first click was 5+ minutes after send, rescue as human.
2. **Timing rescue:** If the first click was 1+ hour after send AND total clicks are under 15, rescue as human even without ESP flag.

**Threshold:** `bot_volume_min_total_clicks` (default: 10).

### BOT:cron_burst *(high confidence, probability: 8)*

**Signal:** Multiple URLs clicked in a tight burst shortly after send, with regular inter-click timing.

**Calibration:** Assigned 8%, actual 2.9%. Slightly over-confident but acceptable.

**Threshold:** `bot_cron_max_inter_click` (default: 10s), `bot_cron_max_time_after_send` (default: 120s).

### HUMAN:esp_confirmed *(high confidence, probability: 88)*

**Signal:** The ESP flagged at least one click as "real" (non-bot) and no bot rules fired.

**Calibration:** Assigned 88%, actual 100% for the sailthru_confirmed variant. Under-confident.

### HUMAN:delayed_single *(definitive confidence, probability: 95)*

**Signal:** Exactly one URL clicked, with significant delay after send (default: 5+ minutes).

**Logic:** The classic human reading pattern. Person receives the email, reads it, clicks the one link that interested them. This is the largest human click category.

**Calibration:** Assigned 95%, actual 93.7%. Extremely well-calibrated.

**Threshold:** `human_delayed_min_seconds` (default: 300s / 5 minutes).

### HUMAN:late_arrival *(high confidence, probability: 90)*

**Signal:** Click occurred 4+ hours after send.

**Calibration:** Assigned 90%, actual 94.1%. Well-calibrated, slightly under-confident.

**Threshold:** `human_late_min_seconds` (default: 14,400s / 4 hours).

### HUMAN:single_selective *(high confidence, probability: 85)*

**Signal:** One URL clicked, 2-60 minutes after send.

**Calibration:** Assigned 85%, actual 94.7%. Under-confident — could be raised.

**Threshold:** `human_selective_min_seconds` (default: 120s), `human_selective_max_seconds` (default: 3,600s).

### UNCLASSIFIED:ambiguous *(low confidence, probability: 30)*

**Signal:** Click pattern does not match any rule above.

**Calibration:** Assigned 30%, actual 60% for human-classified, 10.2% for bot-classified. This bucket needs further decomposition in future versions.

## Rules Removed in V3

### ~~HUMAN:thoughtful_multi~~ *(REMOVED)*

**Was:** Multiple clicks with 60+ second average inter-click time and 3 or fewer URLs.

**Why removed:** 100% false positive rate. Actual human rate: 0%. What appeared to be "thoughtful" multi-clicking was actually delayed bot re-scans happening days apart. The time gaps were too long (average 87 minutes) because they were separate bot scanning sessions, not a human reading through an email.

### ~~HUMAN:single_moderate~~ *(REMOVED)*

**Was:** One URL clicked, 1-5 minutes after send.

**Why removed:** 100% false positive rate. Actual human rate: 0%. Bots with a consistent 2-minute delay clustered in a narrow 121-131 second timing band. This was a specific bot infrastructure with a built-in delay designed to evade simple timing filters.

## Confidence Levels

| Level | Meaning | Typical FP Rate |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| **definitive** | Near-certain. Multiple strong signals agree. | < 1% |
| **high** | Strong signal. One clear indicator. | 1-5% |
| **medium** | Moderate signal. Pattern-based inference. | 3-10% |
| **low** | Weak signal. Insufficient evidence. | 10-30% |

---

## Probability Scores

Each send receives a probability score from 0 to 100:

- **90-100:** Almost certainly human (delayed_single, late_arrival, verified_clicker)
- **70-89:** Likely human (esp_confirmed, single_selective, pattern open rules)
- **40-69:** Uncertain (no_evidence opens, ambiguous clicks)
- **10-39:** Likely bot (never_verified_fast, ambiguous-leaning-bot)
- **0-9:** Almost certainly bot (instant_prefetch, machinegun)
